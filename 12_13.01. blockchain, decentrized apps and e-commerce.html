Lesson 1: Blockchain Architecture

A blockchain is an append-only, cryptographically chained, distributed ledger. This section breaks down its components, network types, and the real-world mechanics of how transactions are processed probabilistically.

The Four Ingredients

Nothing in blockchain is genuinely new. The combination is what is new.

  • 1.
    Cryptographic hash functions Fingerprints the data (e.g., SHA-256).
  • 2.
    Public-key cryptography Sign transactions, prove ownership privately.
  • 3.
    P2P networking No central server, nodes gossip directly.
  • 4.
    Consensus mechanism Rules to decide canonical history (PoW/PoS).

Real-Life Frame: The Madrid Tab

Imagine six friends sharing a tapas tab in Madrid. How do you keep an honest ledger when one friend keeps "forgetting" their orders?

"You create an append-only notebook. Every new page references the hash of the previous page. If someone alters a past order, the math breaks, and anyone re-running the math detects the tamper."

Bitcoin is just this notebook scaled to the planet.

Gas & Smart Contracts

A smart contract is a vending machine for software: deterministic, public, and impossible to argue with. It only runs when triggered by a transaction.

Why GAS exists:

  1. Pays validators for CPU/memory expenditure.
  2. Prevents infinite loops by enforcing a hard computation budget.

No gas = no execution.

Code is Law (The Dark Side)

Smart contracts are immutable. Bugs become permanent vulnerabilities until patched via complex proxy upgrade patterns. The 2016 DAO hack proved that "Code is Law" in production means bugs cost real money.

DApp Categories

Real-Life Frame: Rent Escrow

"Pisos Compartidos": Four students sharing an apartment. Instead of trusting one person or the landlord with the deposit, a smart-contract escrow holds the funds. Rent is paid to the contract. At lease-end, it releases the deposit automatically unless a flag is raised. It is a clearer set of rules, written in code, agreed upon in advance.

Lesson 3: E-Commerce Issues

The biggest invisible industry. Any digital commercial domain must navigate seven persistent structural and operational issues. If a vendor only talks about payments and security, they are ignoring five other massive engineering challenges.

The 7 Persistent Issues

✓ Where Blockchain Actually Helps

  • • Cross-border payments in volatile regions.
  • • Provenance tracking for high-value goods (pharma, luxury).
  • • Loyalty programs surviving brand acquisitions.
  • • Digital ownership of in-game items/certificates.

✗ Where Blockchain Does Not Help

  • • Standard card-based EU retail.
  • • Fast checkout for low-value goods.
  • • Customer-service workflows.
  • • Anywhere a DB + signed audit logs does the job.
