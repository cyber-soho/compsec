Core Concepts

Understanding the foundational definitions, the CIA triad, and the relationship between threats and risks is the first step in your security journey.

What is Computer Security?

Also known as InfoSec, it is the discipline of protecting computing systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, or disruption. It sits at the intersection of technology, human behavior, policy, and law.

Frameworks Explained

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

A voluntary guidance based on existing standards to help organizations manage cybersecurity risk. Used widely in US and industry.

ISO/IEC 27001

International standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). Focuses on process and certification.

Personal Threat Model

Ask yourself these 5 questions to build your model:

  1. Assets: What do you have that is valuable? (Data, Accounts)
  2. Threats: Who wants it and why?
  3. Paths: How would they get in? (Phishing, weak password)
  4. Controls: What stops them? (2FA, Encryption)
  5. Gaps: What are you missing?
