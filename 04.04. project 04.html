Week 1 Learning Objectives

  • Explain network scanning & the kill-chain model.
  • Distinguish host discovery, port scanning, and OS fingerprinting.
  • Apply scanning tools (e.g., Nmap) in a controlled lab.
  • Classify attacks using MITRE or STRIDE.
  • Conduct vulnerability assessment & propose remediation.

Step E: Reflection & Cleanup

Checklist before shutdown:

  • Have you documented all open ports?
  • Did you save screenshots of key findings?
  • Did you test your remediation idea (conceptually)?
  • Action: Shut down VMs safely.

Reflection Prompts:

Report Builder

Your deliverable is a 4-page PDF. Use this structure to ensure you meet all grading criteria.

PG 1

Introduction & Environment

Objectives, Topology, Tools selected.

Tip: Don't just list tools; explain why you chose them. Include your topology diagram.
PG 2

Scanning Results & Analysis

Port lists, Service versions, Attack surface.

Tip: Use tables for open ports. Analyze filtered ports – why are they filtered? Firewall?
PG 3

Classification & Findings

MITRE mapping, Top 3 Vulnerabilities (CVE/CVSS).

Tip: Demonstrate understanding of CVSS vectors, don't just copy the number. Map specific attacks to discovered services.
PG 4

Remediation & Reflection

Specific fixes, Critical reflection.

Tip: Be concrete. Instead of "update system", say "Update vsftpd to version 2.3.5 or disable if unused."
