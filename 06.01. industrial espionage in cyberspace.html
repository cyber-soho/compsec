The covert, unauthorized collection of confidential business or government information. It's not science fiction—it is the theft of time, decades of R&D, and competitive advantage costing the global economy $600 billion USD per year.

The Threat Landscape: Actors & Methods

Understanding the enemy is the first step in defense. This section breaks down who conducts these attacks (from state-sponsored groups to compromised insiders) and the specific, multi-phased lifecycle they use to infiltrate networks. Interact with the Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) timeline to understand how an attack unfolds over months or years.

🏛️

Nation-State Actors

Government-sponsored with military or intelligence backing. Goals include military superiority and economic dominance.

Examples: APT10, Lazarus Group, Cozy Bear
🏢

Corporate Competitors

Entities that hire hackers or place moles. Goals include stealing pricing, product designs, and client lists prior to launch.

Motive: Direct market advantage
👤

Insider Threats

Individuals with authorized access. Can be malicious (revenge/money), negligent (mistakes), or compromised (blackmailed).

Advantage: Keys to the front door

The 5 Phases of an APT Campaign

Common Attack Vectors

Spear Phishing

Targeted email exploiting trust. The attacker researches the victim first. 91% of attacks begin here.

Watering Hole Attacks

Poisoning websites frequently visited by the target community. The prey comes to the trap.

Supply Chain Attacks

Attacking a trusted supplier to reach the real target via legitimate updates (e.g., SolarWinds).

Zero-Day Exploits

Vulnerabilities unknown to the vendor. Bought and stockpiled like weapons by nation-states.

TEMPEST / Physical

Capturing electromagnetic emissions from electronics outside a building to reconstruct data.

Defenses & Zero Trust Architecture

Traditional "castle and moat" security fails when the attacker spends months inside the walls. Modern defense relies on Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA): "Never trust. Always verify." Layered Defense Stack

  • DLP (Data Loss Prevention) Monitors and blocks unauthorized data movement based on content, file type, or abnormal volume.
  • Software Supply Chain Security Verifying vendor updates via SBOMs (Software Bill of Materials) and Hash verification.
  • Threat Intelligence Proactively collecting data on adversary TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures) using frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK.
  • Human Defenses Brief, regular micro-training and building a culture of reporting. A trained employee is worth more than a $1M security tool.

⚖️ The Attribution & Legal Problem

Even "high confidence" attribution is rarely sufficient for criminal prosecution due to obfuscation (VPNs, TOR, false flags). International cyber law is decades behind.

  • US: CFAA (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act)
  • US: EEA (Economic Espionage Act)
  • EU: NIS2 Directive & GDPR
  • Intl: Budapest Convention
